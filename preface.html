Preface

Craft Clusters of India is an initiative of Development Commissioner (DC), Handicrafts, Ministry of Textiles, Government of India to showcase different products made by Cluster Artisans across the country. Artisans are provided with technical/ marketing know-hows for making these products by implementing agencies which are partnered by DC Handicrafts for the upliftment of these artisans.


Terracotta is one of many crafts supported by the Cluster Initiative. Terra- cotta is a traditional form of clay-based unglazed ceramic. It has been used throughout history for sculpture and pottery, as well as bricks and roof shingles. The term is also used to refer to items made out of this material and to its natural, brownish orange colour. For more than ten thousand years man has expressed his feelings and his aesthetic in clay. According to a myth, it was an accidental fire which provided the knowledge that clay hardened on firing it to a certain temperature.


As a part of our curriculum, we, the students of the Department of Fashion Communication, National Institute of Fashion Technology, Chennai, in the midst of this unpredictable pandemic, sat down and spent a few online sessions with the artisans of Rajan Art & Crafts, Chennai, to learn, study and analyse the beauty of this craft.
