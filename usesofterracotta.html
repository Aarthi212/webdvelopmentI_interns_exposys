Uses of Terracotta

1)Terracotta Pots (Cooking)-
Terracotta is type of fired clay which makes it perfect for longer duration cooking over low heat. Terracotta pots are used for slow cooking, roasting, frying, baking and boiling. Using terracotta cookware is a 'green' way of cooking as it preserves all the nutrients and flavours and is an excellent retainer of heat. Using terracotta kitchenware also give your kitchen a very natural, warm, welcoming and a retro vibe.
2) Terracotta Utensils (Storage)-

Terracotta utensils were used for storing water, cooked vegetables and many other items by our elders. Not only are terracotta utensils great for storage, they also have lots of properties which make it a perfect choice for kitchenware products.
• Nutrient value- The minerals in terracotta get mixed in the stored food / liquid while keeping its natural nutrients intact hence increasing the nutrient value of the dish.
• Microwave safe- plastic containers are generally used for heating purposes in the microwave. People are Innova on negligence towards the fact that plastic produces highly toxic gases and chemicals when heated and these chemicals get mixed up with the food. On the other hand, using terracotta products is organic and good for our bodies.
• Ideal for storing dairy products- Bengali dishes such as Mishti doi are stored in terracotta clay pots. The secret behind the unique and fabulous taste of the curd is the rawness and earthiness of terracotta.




4) Terracotta Tiles-

Terracotta tiles were used centuries ago in many old homes and buildings in Europe. Reclaimed terracotta tiles are durable and resistant to water and fire which make it perfect for flooring. These days glazed terracotta is also used which increases the longevity of these tiles. Few advantages of terracotta tiles are-
• Aesthetics- Terracotta tiles range from yellows to reds and browns. These warm hues bring an earthy and welcoming feel to the home. It creates a very pleasing and inviting environment.
• Unique and authentic- these styles reflect the beauty of culture and bring a unique, old world aesthetics to your home.
• Environment friendly- terracotta tiles require very light energy resources for manufacturing which not only makes them environment friendly, but also greatly reduces the cost of manufacturing.
• Long lasting- these styles can withstand a lot of wear and tear and are great for flooring especially in heavy trafficked areas.

5) Terracotta in Gardens-

Using terracotta in gardens offers a whole range of advantages over other materials. Over-watering is not a problem with terracotta pots as they are porous in nature. Due to the poorest nature of the material, the excess water evaporates easily and prevents water logging which can damage the roots of sensitive plants.
Terracotta containers and pots are available in lots of shapes and sizes making them suitable for all types of plants, big or small. They come in attractive designs, are very lightweight and easy to handle.
Not every plant will benefit from terracotta material. It is heavy, cracks easily, and gets a white crusty film over time. However, for plants such as succulents and cacti, it is an excellent container. Since the planters dry out quickly, plants that are in full sun may become too dry. The material isn’t good for seedlings or plants like some ferns, which need consistently moist soil.








6) Terracotta Jewellery-

Terracotta jewellery is one of the oldest forms of jewellery in the world. Evidences of earrings, ear studs, necklaces, pendants, bangles and bracelets made from terracotta have been found by archaeologists in the Harappan and Mohenjodaro civilizations. Since this jewellery is made out of natural clay, it is very skin-friendly and doesn’t cause any rashes or skin irritation. It is very affordable and also gives a very boho chick feel. As it is made of clay, terracotta jewellery is prone to breakage and thus it needs to be taken care of properly.
Terracotta jewellery has a rustic and an earthen appeal. The process of making involves purifying the clay, shaping and designing the wet slab. After drying it completely it is fired in a kiln at a very high temperature to give it a stone consistency. When clay is fired, it can take a natural brick colour or shades of brown, pink, or even white, depending on the quality of clay. Bindhu Mathai of Aakaar from Bangalore ,Hema Subramaniam of Alankriti also of Bangalore are some terracotta jewellery designers.


7) Home decor-

Terracotta is one of the oldest art forms which is still prevalent in the modern era. Home decor products are made out of terracotta as it brings an early tone inside the house which makes the environment very soothing and appealing. The bright shades of orange, red, and brown create the right contrast in any room. Moreover, Terracotta home decor products are available in many exciting shapes and forms and creative designs. Terracotta home decor products these days are a perfect blend of modern and classic styles.
Terracotta can complement many interior design styles, from the modern to the boho. With its warm, calming effect and its ability to combine perfectly with a whole range of different colors, this color is becoming more and more popular in the world of interior design. Terracotta is great for creating feelings of warmth, comfort, and well-being. When it comes to combining terracotta with other colors, browns are a great option. Alternatively, whites, grays, and dark greens can also work really well.
